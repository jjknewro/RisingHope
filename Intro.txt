1. MISSION STATEMENT 
The mission of Rising Hope Tikvah Olah Foundation, Inc. is to provide hope, opportunity, and compassionate support to students, individuals, and families facing financial and emotional challenges. Through educational assistance, mental health services, advocacy, and community partnerships, the Foundation works to remove barriers to learning, healing, and personal growth so every individual can thrive with dignity, resilience, and hope.
2. VISION STATEMENT
Rising Hope Tikvah Olah Foundation envisions a community where every child, student, individual, and family has access to quality education, emotional wellness resources, and compassionate support regardless of financial circumstances.

3. CORE VALUES
Compassion
We treat every individual with dignity, kindness, empathy, and respect.
Hope
We believe access to education and mental health support can transform lives.
Integrity
We commit to ethical stewardship, transparency, and accountability.
Inclusion
We serve individuals and families of all backgrounds without discrimination.
Community
We believe meaningful partnerships strengthen families and communities.

4. ARTICLES OF INCORPORATION
ARTICLES OF INCORPORATION
OF
Rising Hope Tikvah Olah Foundation, Inc.
ARTICLE I — NAME
The name of the corporation is Rising Hope Tikvah Olah Foundation, Inc.
ARTICLE II — CORPORATE STATUS
This corporation is a nonprofit corporation organized exclusively for charitable and educational purposes under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code.
ARTICLE III — PURPOSE
The corporation is organized exclusively for charitable and educational purposes including:
Providing educational assistance, scholarships, tutoring, enrichment opportunities, and advancement programs for students and families experiencing financial hardship;
Supporting individuals and families requiring mental health assistance, therapy, counseling, psychological services, and wellness support;
Raising and distributing funds for educational and mental health support services;
Providing advocacy, outreach, and awareness programs;
Engaging in any lawful activities permitted to organizations exempt under Section 501(c)(3).
ARTICLE IV — NONPROFIT OPERATION
No part of the net earnings of the corporation shall benefit any private individual except reasonable compensation for services rendered.
ARTICLE V — DISSOLUTION
Upon dissolution, assets shall be distributed to one or more organizations qualifying under Section 501(c)(3).

6. CONFLICT OF INTEREST POLICY
Any director, officer, or committee member with a financial or personal interest in a transaction or arrangement involving the Foundation must disclose the existence of the conflict.
The interested person shall not participate in discussions or voting related to the matter.
The Board shall determine whether a conflict exists and whether the transaction is fair and in the Foundation’s best interest.

10. MENTAL HEALTH ASSISTANCE POLICY
Purpose
The Foundation may provide financial assistance and referrals for therapy, counseling, psychological services, and wellness support.
Confidentiality
All applicant information shall remain confidential except where disclosure is required by law.
Referral Network
The Foundation may partner with licensed therapists, counselors, psychologists, social workers, schools, and community organizations.
Crisis Situations
The Foundation does not provide emergency mental health services. Individuals in crisis shall be referred to emergency services or crisis hotlines.
